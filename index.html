The Zochonis Charitable Trust (ZCT) was founded by Sir John Zochonis in 1977 to assist charities operating in the North West of England. It was funded by Sir John donating a considerable number of his own PZ Cussons shares to the Trust. In its first year, the Trust made just a single donation of £6,750 which was to The University of Manchester. Since then, as more shares were bequeathed by members of the Zochonis family and as PZ Cussons dividends have increased, that figure has risen substantially. Currently around thirteen percent of the company’s dividends go to the Trust.

The focus remains to support charities working in the North West of England, although a portion goes to help charities operating in West Africa. In the main, the Trust assists young people, particularly in the spheres of education, health and social provision. ZCT donates to a wide variety of charitable causes which range from national charities with operations in the North West of England to much smaller, regional organisations which work to meet local needs and problems. The sizes of donations range from substantial sums to lesser amounts which are nonetheless very welcome.

Over the past forty years a number of people have given their time selflessly to assist with the Trust and a debt of gratitude is owed to them. Currently, ZCT is run by three Trustees and a Management Assistant, Marie Gallagher. Between them, the four ZCT team members have nearly half a century’s experience working with the Trust. Annual operating costs are low, at less than two percent of the amount given to charity.
